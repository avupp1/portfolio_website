Lunar Pit and Cave Explorer (L’PACE)

Sep 2024 - Dec 2024

A conceptual lunar mission designed to explore the Marius Hills pit region and assess its potential for future human habitation. The project features a dual-rover system: a Surface Rover for terrain mapping and support, and a Subsurface Rover for cave exploration. Together, they collect data on radiation levels, temperature stability, structural integrity, and the presence of volatiles such as water ice. This mission aims to advance our understanding of lunar lava tubes as potential safe habitats for long-duration human exploration.

On-Site Refueling Device - Universal Magnetic Docking Interface

Jan 2025 - Apr 2025

A conceptual spacecraft refueling system designed to advance in-orbit propellant transfer for future deep-space missions. The project develops a Universal Magnetic Docking Interface (UMDI) that replaces traditional mechanical berthing mechanisms with a self-aligning magnetic coupling system, enabling contactless and efficient fluid transfer. By reducing mass, minimizing mechanical wear, and improving cross-compatibility between spacecraft, the design enhances reliability and scalability for orbital fuel depots. This innovation supports sustained satellite servicing, Artemis missions, and long-duration lunar and Mars exploration architectures.

Arduino Temperature Alarm System

Apr 2025

A microcontroller-based temperature monitoring system designed to detect and respond to thermal threshold conditions in real time. The project integrates an Arduino platform with calibrated thermistors, protective housing, and supporting circuit components to ensure accurate and reliable temperature sensing. Control logic was developed in the Arduino IDE to trigger alarm responses based on predefined limits. System performance was validated across varying temperature conditions, with documented calibration data supporting measurement consistency and future design refinement.

Portable Refracting Telescope & Tripod System Design

Dec 2025

A portable refracting telescope and collapsible tripod system engineered to provide smooth focusing performance and enhanced vibration stability. The project involved collaborative mechanical design and modeling of fifteen custom components in SolidWorks, supported by detailed engineering drawings to validate manufacturability and transportability. Structural load analysis and finite element analysis (FEA) were conducted to verify tripod stability under a 65 lbf applied load, achieving a safety factor of 2.0. The final design balanced optical performance, structural integrity, and portability within defined engineering constraints.

Anisotropic Stiffness Analysis - E-Glass Composite Lamina

Jan 2026

A computational study of stress and strain behavior in a unidirectional E-Glass composite lamina under in-plane loading at varying material orientations. Classical lamination theory and stress-strain transformation matrices were applied to evaluate responses in both global and material coordinate systems. Two independent solution methods were implemented and verified at a control angle (θ = 72.6°), confirming analytical consistency. Parametric analysis across the angular range demonstrated strong anisotropic behavior, nonlinear strain variation, and coupling between normal and shear components, emphasizing the importance of fiber orientation in tailoring composite performance.
